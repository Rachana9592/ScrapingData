​​Xylella fastidiosa /SiteCollectionImages/pests-diseases-weeds/1-xylella.jpg /pests-diseases-weeds/plant/xylella Americas Bacterium Containment is critical. This could be as easy as bagging a suspect plant specimen that has symptoms or stopping an insect escaping by closing the doors on a shipping container. Controlling vectors and removing infected plant material are the only control options, in conjunction with movement restrictions on infected plant material, to limit its spread. Identification and use of resistant plant stock may provide a long term management option, as the disease would be nearly impossible to eradicate.


Khapra beetle		 /SiteCollectionImages/pests-diseases-weeds/2-khapra.jpg /pests-diseases-weeds/plant/khapra-beetle India Insect Containment is critical. Khapra beetles do not fly, but can  be readily moved around in infested goods. Try to locate the source of the  infestation and secure it by sealing in a plastic bag or restricting access to  the area.


Fruit flies		 /SiteCollectionImages/pests-diseases-weeds/3-exotic-fruit-fly.jpg /pests-diseases-weeds/plant/fruit-flies Asia Insect Containment is critical. Fruit fly adults are active fliers.  Try to locate the source of the infestation and secure the fruit by it sealing  in a plastic bag.  Do not move infested fruit or vegetables from the orchard or  your yard.

Giant African snail		 /SiteCollectionImages/pests-diseases-weeds/9-giant-african-snail.jpg /pests-diseases-weeds/plant/giant-african-snail Varies, depending on the species Snail Containment is critical. Inspect the area where you found the snail and try  to trace it to the source.

Leaf miner		 /SiteCollectionImages/pests-diseases-weeds/20-leaf-miner.jpg /pests-diseases-weeds/plant/leaf-miner Containment is critical. If you work around imported goods or at a port or airport, report any bee swarms. If you see bees that you don’t think are European honey bees, or suspicious hives, take a photo to help with identification, and secure the site if possible. Bees can be dangerous to humans, so do not attempt to interfere with them or their nests.
